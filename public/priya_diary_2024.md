Personal Diary of Priya Sharma
================================
Written by Priya, for Priya.

---

January 6, 2024 — Priya's first entry of the year

My name is Priya and I'm starting this journal because I feel like I've been living too fast and thinking too little.

2024 feels important. I can't explain why. I'm 24, I'm working at a company that's fine but not exciting, and I have this nagging feeling that I'm capable of more than I'm letting myself do.

The thing I want most this year: to be brave. Not dramatically brave. Just brave enough to apply for things. Brave enough to say what I actually think. Brave enough to go somewhere alone.

Let's see who Priya becomes this year.

— Priya

---

February 15, 2024 — Priya writes after a hard day

Yesterday was awful. My manager Rohit shot down my entire presentation in front of the team. I had worked on it for two weeks. Two weeks, and he dismissed it in about 90 seconds.

I cried in the bathroom afterward. I won't pretend I didn't.

But here's what I know about myself: Priya doesn't give up. Never has. So I came home, opened my laptop, and rebuilt the whole thing. Better structure. Clearer reasoning. A stronger conclusion.

I'm not doing this to prove something to Rohit. I'm doing it to prove something to myself.

Tomorrow Priya presents again. And this time, she's ready.

---

March 22, 2024 — THE DAY

I got the internship.

Priya Sharma got the internship at the design studio.

I've been staring at that email for an hour. It's real. It's actually real.

I called Meera first. She screamed so loud I had to hold the phone away from my ear. Then I called Mom. She cried. Dad asked about the stipend and I laughed for the first time all day.

This is the moment I'll look back on. I know it.

— Priya, 3:45 PM, the best day of March 2024

---

May 10, 2024 — Priya reflects on the internship

Three weeks in. Some observations:

1. I am learning so much it's overwhelming in the best way
2. My mentor Ananya is brilliant and terrifying simultaneously
3. I cried once this week (at my desk, very discreetly)
4. I also had my best design session ever on Thursday
5. I think I belong here

Priya's verdict: hard, worth it, stay.

---

July 18, 2024 — The night before Goa

Priya is going to Goa tomorrow. Alone.

I booked this trip three weeks ago in a moment of courage and I have been both excited and quietly panicking ever since.

I've never traveled alone. Not even for one night. The idea that I'll be entirely responsible for myself — no plans, no group, no one to coordinate with — is both thrilling and a little terrifying.

Meera said: "Come back softer, Priya. You deserve it."

I want to come back softer.

Priya, signing off before the adventure

---

July 21, 2024 — Goa, Day 3

Something shifted today.

Priya woke up at 5:30 AM without an alarm. Walked to the beach alone. Watched the sun rise over the water. Sat with chai for an hour. No phone. No agenda. No one waiting for me.

I felt — peaceful. Genuinely, deeply peaceful.

I think I've been running on stress for so long that I forgot what it feels like to just exist. This trip reminded me. Priya needed this more than she knew.

I talked to a retired couple at dinner tonight. They've been traveling for eight months. They told me: "The life you want is always closer than you think. You just have to move toward it."

I'm moving, I think. Slowly. But moving.

— Priya, sunburned and happy

---

October 20, 2024 — A quiet night

Priya is tired tonight. Not physically — emotionally.

Work is fine. Life is fine. But "fine" sometimes feels like its own kind of heavy.

I've been thinking about what I actually want. Not just in career terms, but in life. I want deep conversations and long walks and work that makes me feel alive. I want to feel like I'm moving toward something.

Meera checks in on me even when I go quiet. That's the kind of friendship that keeps you going. I don't take it lightly.

Priya's reminder to self: rest is not the same as giving up.

---

December 31, 2024 — Last page

This is Priya's last entry of 2024.

What a year. I genuinely didn't know this version of me existed twelve months ago.

I got the internship. I went to Goa alone. I cried and recovered and showed up anyway. I learned to say what I need. I learned that rest is allowed. I learned that the people who stay when things are hard — Meera, Mom, Dad — are the ones who matter most.

2025, Priya is ready.

Not because everything is figured out. But because I finally trust myself to figure it out.

— Priya Sharma, 11:59 PM
