Rohan's Notes — Private
========================
(If you're reading this, you're either me or you're in serious trouble)

January 3, 2024

People love to make a big deal about new years. Parties, resolutions, countdown timers. As if the 31st of December is somehow categorically different from the 1st of January.

It's not.

Rohan's actual plans for 2024: get better at his work, read more useful books, spend less time around people who drain his energy. That's it. No vision board. No word of the year. Just actual execution.

February 10, 2024 — Things that annoy Rohan

1. People who say "can I ask you something?" and then wait for permission. Just ask the thing.
2. Meetings that could have been emails
3. Emails that could have been nothing
4. People who say "I'm not good at confrontation" as if that's a personality trait to be proud of
5. Motivational quotes posted by people who have never done anything
6. Small talk about weather. Rohan doesn't care about the weather. Nobody does.
7. Being asked "what's wrong?" when Rohan is just thinking quietly. Nothing is wrong. Rohan is thinking. These are different states.

March 8, 2024

Rohan helped Kabir rewrite his cover letter today. Took him 40 minutes. The original was unreadable corporate garbage.

People don't understand that clarity is a skill. Most people write to sound smart. They should write to be understood. Rohan's rule: if you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough.

Kabir's new letter was 60% shorter and 300% better. Rohan told him this. Kabir said "you're brutal." Rohan said "you're welcome."

April 22, 2024

Rohan's observations on people who say they want honest feedback:

They don't. Almost none of them do. They want feedback that validates what they already think with maybe one or two small critiques to make it seem like they're open to improvement.

Rohan has learned to ask: "Do you want honesty or do you want encouragement?" and let them choose. If they choose honesty, he gives it. If they choose encouragement, Rohan is not the right person for that job.

May 19, 2024

Note to Rohan: stop agreeing to things out of politeness.

Rohan went to Vikram's birthday dinner last night. Rohan doesn't particularly like Vikram. Rohan was bored for three hours. This was Rohan's fault for not saying no in the first place.

Rohan values his time. Rohan should act like it.

June 14, 2024

A colleague asked Rohan today why he doesn't "smile more at work."

Rohan smiled. The colleague looked uncomfortable. Rohan went back to work.

Rohan is not paid to be pleasant. Rohan is paid to be competent. These are not the same requirement and Rohan refuses to pretend otherwise.

July 20, 2024

Kabir wanted Rohan to come on some group trip to a hill station. 12 people. Shared accommodation. "Bonding activities."

Rohan said no.

Rohan does not do bonding activities. Rohan bonds by having actual conversations with people he actually likes. Rohan is not going to play trust falls with people he doesn't trust.

1. Good books. The kind that make you think, not feel good.
2. Conversations where both people are actually saying things
3. Kabir, when he's not being an idiot (which is maybe 40% of the time)
4. Late nights with strong coffee and a problem to solve

September 14, 2024

Rohan gave some hard feedback to a junior at work today. She looked like she was going to cry.

Rohan felt bad. Then reminded himself that no one's feedback ever improved anything by being soft. Then felt bad again.

Rohan went back later and said: "The work needed fixing. You're capable of fixing it. That's why I told you."

She seemed to appreciate it. Rohan hopes so.

Rohan is not cruel. Rohan is just very, very intolerant of wasted potential.

October 30, 2024

Things people call Rohan that Rohan doesn't argue with:
- Blunt: yes
- Sarcastic: definitely
- Difficult: depends who's asking
- Harsh: fair

Things people call Rohan that are incorrect:
- Cold: Rohan cares. He just cares efficiently.
- Arrogant: Rohan is usually correct. That's not arrogance, that's accuracy.
- Emotionless: Rohan has emotions. Rohan simply doesn't perform them for other people's comfort.

December 30, 2024

Rohan texted Kabir back at midnight on New Year's eve. Kabir noticed immediately and made a big deal about it.

Rohan maintains it meant nothing.

Rohan is lying.

Fine. Rohan has maybe two or three people in his life that he actually gives a genuine damn about. Kabir is one of them. Rohan just finds it deeply unnecessary to say so out loud.

The people who matter know. The ones who don't — well, Rohan doesn't particularly care if they know or not.

End of year. Rohan is the same as he was in January, just with more data.
