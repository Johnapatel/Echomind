# My Diary — 2024

---

## January 4, 2024

New year. New page. I always do this — buy a journal, write in it for a week, then forget it exists. But this year feels different somehow. There's a quiet kind of excitement sitting in my chest that I can't fully explain.

I've been thinking about what I actually want. Not what I'm supposed to want, not what would make my parents proud or what looks good on paper — what *I* actually want, deep down.

I want to create things that matter. I want to travel somewhere I've never been, alone, with just a backpack and no plan. I want to wake up excited about what the day holds.

I think 2024 is going to ask something of me. I just have to be brave enough to answer.

---

## February 3, 2024

Bad week. Really bad week.

I don't even want to write about it in detail because writing makes things feel more real and I'm not ready for that. But the short version: I felt completely invisible at work. Like my ideas don't land, like I speak and no one really hears. It's an awful feeling — being in a room full of people and feeling utterly alone.

Called Mama in the evening. She didn't try to fix it or tell me what to do. She just said "I know, beta. It's hard. But you're harder." 

I needed that more than any advice.

---

## February 28, 2024

Something shifted today.

I was sitting in a coffee shop, working on my portfolio, and I realized — I was enjoying it. Like genuinely, quietly enjoying the act of making something. I got so lost in it that I missed lunch.

I don't know why that felt significant. Maybe because it's been a long time since work felt like play. Maybe because I needed proof that the love for it is still there, even when things are hard.

It's still there. That matters.

---

## March 22, 2024

I got the internship. I GOT THE INTERNSHIP.

I'm writing this sitting on my bed at 3pm having done absolutely nothing productive since reading the email at 11am because I've just been sitting with this feeling.

Is this what it feels like when something you worked really hard for actually happens? It's not fireworks. It's more like — warmth. A deep, settled warmth.

Called Mama first. Then Priya. Then Papa who immediately said "so what's the salary" and I laughed so hard.

I'm proud of myself. I don't say that often. But today I am.

---

## May 15, 2024

The internship is intense. I won't sugarcoat it.

Naina (my manager) is brilliant and terrifying in equal measure. She handed back my first draft last week with about 30 comments and said "start over." I cried a little in the bathroom. Then I came back to my desk and started over.

I'm learning what I'm made of. That's the only way I can describe it.

---

## August 18, 2024 — Goa, Day 1

I'm here. I actually did it.

Sitting on the balcony of my little guesthouse, fan spinning overhead, sounds of the sea in the distance. I came alone. I'm slightly terrified and completely alive.

I unpacked, took a shower, walked to the beach, and sat there until the sun went down. Didn't talk to anyone. Didn't check Instagram. Just sat there with the water and my thoughts.

The thought that kept coming back: *Why don't I do this more? Why do I keep waiting for permission to just... be?*

---

## August 20, 2024 — Goa, Day 3

Something happened this morning.

I woke up at 5:30 without an alarm. Walked to the beach before anyone else arrived. Watched the sun come up slowly, everything going from grey to gold. Had chai from a small stall. Sat for an hour doing nothing.

For the first time in as long as I can remember, I felt genuinely, completely peaceful.

I've been running on fumes. Stress and deadlines and trying to be enough. But this morning I was just... a person. Watching the sun rise. And that was enough.

I want to carry this feeling home with me.

---

## October 9, 2024

Had a hard conversation with Arjun today. Long overdue.

I told him I've been feeling like an afterthought lately. That I miss us — the us that used to talk for hours about nothing and everything. He listened. Really listened. Didn't defend himself immediately, didn't make it about him. He just listened.

Afterward he said, "I didn't know you felt that way. I should have asked."

We're okay. We're more than okay actually — I think we just needed to be honest. Honest is scary but it's also the only thing that actually works.

---

## December 20, 2024

End of year.

I'm trying to hold the whole year in my hands and understand it.

I got the internship. I traveled alone for the first time. I had hard conversations that needed to happen. I cried and recovered and tried again. I made things I'm genuinely proud of.

I started this year wanting to be brave enough to answer when life called.

I think I was.

---

## December 31, 2024

Last page of this journal. Last page of this year.

To whoever I am next year: be kinder to yourself. Trust yourself more. Do the brave thing faster.

You're doing okay. You really are.
